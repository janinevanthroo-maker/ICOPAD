Three "Head Spear" Roles at the Belgian Federal Level

Translated from the 44-page strategic summary — linking your core expertise in education and technology to political leadership within the Belgian federal government.

Strategic Intent

These three roles represent the sharpest strategic leverage points — the areas where your expertise in education and technology most directly intersects with Belgian federal government priorities, creating opportunities to lead rather than advise.

The Three Roles

1
Federal Architect of Educational Technology
FOD Onderwijs / SPF Education — Digital Policy & Innovation

The claim: Position yourself as the architect of next-generation EdTech infrastructure at the Belgian federal level — the person who bridges the gap between what educational technology can do and what current federal policy enables.

Why you are credible: Most federal education officials understand policy but not technology. Most technologists understand systems but not pedagogy or public procurement. You sit at the intersection.

  • Operationally define "digital equity" at the Belgian federal level — not just in policy documents
  • Shape how AI tools are evaluated, approved and deployed in Belgian educational institutions
  • Build the federal framework for EdTech standards that Communities must meet to access federal funding
  • Coordinate with the three Communities (Flemish, French, German-speaking) for a coherent educational digital vision

Entry point: Advisory role to SPF Education → contribution to federal digital education regulation → senior appointed or policy adviser position.

2
Federal Lead for Digital Skills Strategy
SPF Employment / SPF Economy (FOD Economie) — Future of Work Initiative

The claim: Own the Belgian federal narrative around preparing the workforce for technological disruption — particularly workers whose jobs will be redefined by AI and automation in the next decade.

Why you are credible: This role demands simultaneous mastery of labour markets, technology adoption curves and educational pathway design. It is not a policy role — it is a systems design role that requires exactly your combination of expertise.

  • Design the federal reskilling architecture: who is eligible, which programmes count, how outcomes are measured
  • Define what a "technology-ready qualification" means at the Belgian federal level
  • Build public-private partnerships between training centres, tech companies and federal public services (SPF)
  • Align with the National AI Convergence Plan (FPS BOSA) and the Belgian National Recovery and Resilience Plan

Entry point: SPF Employment working group on digital skills → National Labour Council (CNT) → Federal Parliamentary Committee on the Digital Economy.

3
Federal Commissioner for AI Literacy & Ethics
SPF BOSA / Council of Ministers — Public Trust in Digital Systems

The claim: As AI integrates into education, hiring, housing and healthcare, someone must be the Belgian federal voice ensuring the public understands these systems and can engage with them critically. This is an emerging role that does not yet fully exist — you can define it.

Why you are credible: AI ethics without educational expertise produces compliance theatre. Educational reform without AI fluency produces outdated curricula. The synthesis is rare and under-represented at the Belgian federal level.

  • Define Belgian national AI literacy standards for primary, secondary and adult education
  • Lead federal guidance on algorithmic transparency in public-sector AI systems (administrations, CPAS, municipalities)
  • Serve as the public communicator bridging technical AI policy and civic understanding
  • Contribute to Belgium's implementation of the EU AI Act in the education domain

Entry point: Contributor to the National AI Convergence Plan (SPF BOSA) → Technology Fellow within the federal government → Senior appointed adviser to the Prime Minister or Deputy Prime Minister responsible for digital affairs.

The Common Thread

All three roles share the same underlying logic: Belgian federal institutions are making high-stakes technology decisions without sufficient educational perspective, and educational institutions are making high-stakes technology adoptions without sufficient federal coordination. You are positioned to close both gaps simultaneously.

Belgian Federal Strategy Overview

This document outlines the strategic positioning and objectives for engagement at the Belgian federal government level. It is the guiding framework connecting all other strategic documents in this hub.

Strategic Pillars

1. Establish Credibility Through Specificity

Influence at the Belgian federal level is earned through demonstrated expertise in a precise domain, then broadened. The strategy is to become the definitive voice on the education-technology nexus at the federal level before expanding into adjacent policy areas.

2. Build Institutional Relationships Before Appointments

Advisory roles, working groups and federal expert panels are the primary pathway to appointed positions. The strategy prioritises relationships with SPF heads of department, parliamentary committee staff and ministerial cabinets over public advocacy alone.

3. Create the Category, Then Lead It

The most durable federal roles are those that did not exist before the person who claimed them defined their scope. The three head spear roles in this document represent areas where no clearly identified Belgian federal lead currently exists.

Key Belgian Federal Entry Points

  • SPF BOSA (Federal Public Service Strategy and Support) — Cross-departmental digital and AI coordination, National AI Convergence Plan
  • SPF Education, Culture and Research (FOD Onderwijs) — Federal education and training policy, research funding
  • SPF Employment, Labour and Social Dialogue — Federal labour market policy, digital skills, vocational training
  • FOD Economy, SMEs, Middle Classes and Energy — Digital competitiveness strategy, tech industrial policy
  • Federal Parliament — Economy and Education Committee — Primary parliamentary jurisdiction over federal digital and education policies
  • Council of Ministers — Deputy Prime Minister responsible for Digital — Policy coordination at the highest level of the De Wever federal government

Education & Technology — Areas for Action

Core competency document covering the intersection of education policy and technological innovation in Belgium. This is the foundational expertise underpinning all three federal strategic roles.

Why This Intersection is Strategic at the Belgian Federal Level

Belgium faces simultaneous challenges in educational outcomes (per PISA and OECD indicators) and digital economy readiness. The SPFs respond in silos: SPF Education focuses on pedagogy and access, FOD Economy focuses on technological competitiveness, and SPF Employment focuses on labour market outcomes. None of them owns the intersection.

The Central Insight

Technology is redefining what education must accomplish. Education is the only lever that can ensure technology serves everyone. Belgian federal policy has not yet caught up with either reality. That gap is the strategic opportunity.

Education Policy Positions

Digital Equity as Infrastructure

Broadband access, device access and digital literacy are not supplementary educational resources — they are foundational infrastructure equivalent to electricity and plumbing. Belgian federal funding frameworks must reflect this, in coordination with the Communities and Regions.

AI in Belgian Schools: A Decision Framework

Belgian schools need a federal framework for evaluating, procuring and deploying AI tools — one that simultaneously addresses data protection (GDPR), algorithmic bias, pedagogical effectiveness and teacher training. No such coherent framework currently exists at the Belgian federal level.

Certification and Qualification Reform

Federal recognition of alternative credentials — micro-credentials, stackable certificates, competency-based assessments — requires a coherent federal quality framework, linked to the Flemish Qualifications Structure and the French-speaking Qualifications Framework.

Technology Policy Positions

Responsible AI Deployment in Belgian Public Services

When AI systems are used in Belgian public education — for assessment, discipline, admissions or instruction — they constitute de facto public infrastructure and should be subject to transparency, auditability and fairness requirements, in line with the EU AI Act and European Commission guidance.

Technology Public Procurement Reform

Federal and Community public procurement for educational technology suffers from long contracts, vendor lock-in and inadequate evaluation criteria. Reform requires both policy changes and technical standards coordinated across Belgian levels of government.

Alignment with the Belgian National AI Convergence Plan (SPF BOSA, 2022)

Belgium launched its National AI Convergence Plan in 2022, coordinated by SPF BOSA. This plan defines federal AI priorities — ICOPAD strategic roles must align with and contribute to this existing framework.
