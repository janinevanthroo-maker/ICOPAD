Education Programme

ICOPAD Education Thematic Programme

Consultative Discussion on Supporting Parents of African and African Descent Children · June 2026

Active

Background

As we prepare for the implementation of the Second International Decade for People of African Descent (2025–2034), education remains one of the most important areas through which we can contribute to the recognition, development, and empowerment of our communities.

During recent ICOPAD discussions, members shared observations and experiences concerning the educational journeys of children of African and African descent families. Among the issues highlighted were observations from Belgium suggesting that children's educational trajectories are often influenced more strongly by the educational attainment of mothers than fathers. Members also shared experiences from South Asian communities, where public schools are frequently perceived to provide education of equal or better quality than private schools, yet migrant families are often encouraged towards more costly private education options.

These discussions demonstrated the need for a focused initiative that moves beyond identifying challenges and towards developing practical solutions, guidance, and tools that support parents, educators, and children.

Purpose

The purpose of the programme is to bring together parents, educators, researchers, community leaders, policymakers, and other relevant stakeholders to identify key challenges and co-create practical tools that strengthen educational outcomes and parental engagement.

Objectives

  • Identify common challenges faced by parents in understanding and navigating education systems.
  • Examine factors influencing educational outcomes for children of African and African descent.
  • Share experiences, evidence, and good practices from different countries and communities.
  • Develop practical guides, toolkits, and recommendations that parents can use in their everyday engagement with schools and educators.
  • Explore opportunities for collaboration among educators, researchers, community organisations, and policymakers.

Proposed Process

Meeting 1 · Scoping the Issues

  • Sharing experiences and evidence from different countries and communities.
  • Identifying priority challenges and opportunities.
  • Mapping existing resources, initiatives, and gaps.

Meeting 2 · Expert Consultation and Co-Creation

  • Engagement with invited experts and practitioners.
  • Discussion of promising practices and lessons learned.
  • Initial development of practical tools, guides, and recommendations.

Meeting 3 · Validation and Finalisation

  • Review and refinement of draft tools and guidance materials.
  • Agreement on practical next steps and implementation priorities.
  • Identification of opportunities for piloting, dissemination, and partnership development.

Expected Outputs

  • A Parent Navigation Guide for understanding and engaging with education systems.
  • A Parent–Teacher Engagement Toolkit.
  • Practical recommendations for strengthening home–school collaboration.
  • A network of experts and practitioners willing to support future implementation and mentoring initiatives.

Expected Outcomes

  • Increased awareness of educational challenges affecting African and African descent families.
  • Improved parent engagement and confidence in navigating educational systems.
  • Stronger collaboration between parents, schools, and communities.
  • Practical and community-informed tools that can be adapted and used across different contexts.

Conclusion

Education is a critical foundation for the empowerment and advancement of future generations. Through this thematic programme, ICOPAD seeks to facilitate constructive dialogue, shared learning, and the development of practical solutions that support the educational success and well-being of children of African and African descent.

ICOPAD · Towards Practical Solutions for Stronger Educational Outcomes

Internal Circular

Cover Letter — Education Programme Circulation

From Bwalya Mwali, for and on behalf of ICOPAD · June 2026

For Review

Dear ICOPAD Members,

I hope you are all well.

Following our recent discussions and the valuable contributions shared by members, we are pleased to circulate for your review a proposed Education Thematic Programme entitled:

"Consultative Discussion on Supporting Parents of African and African Descent Children in Navigating and Engaging with Their Children's Education"

The proposal responds to concerns and observations raised by members regarding educational experiences within our communities, including factors influencing children's educational trajectories, parental engagement with schools, and the need for practical tools that can support parents, educators, and children.

The programme is envisioned as a series of at least three consultative meetings aimed at bringing together community members, parents, educators, researchers, and other experts to identify challenges, share good practices, and co-create practical guidance and tools.

We kindly invite you to review the attached thematic programme and share any comments, suggestions, or observations that may strengthen the proposed process and expected outcomes.

A separate call for volunteers and expert recommendations will be circulated next week to support the implementation of this initiative.

We look forward to your feedback and continued collaboration as we prepare for the Second International Decade for People of African Descent (2025–2034).

With appreciation,

Bwalya Mwali
For and on behalf of ICOPAD

Federal-Level Strategy

Three Strategic "Head Spear" Roles

These three roles translate ICOPAD's programme summary into distinct, claimable leadership positions at the federal level — each one rooted in ICOPAD's core focus on education and technology, while opening a wider doorway into national policy influence.

01
Head Spear Role #1 · Education Systems Advocate

Federal Voice on Equitable Education for People of African Descent

ICOPAD positions itself as the primary civil-society interlocutor with federal education ministries and agencies on issues affecting African and African Descent families — bringing community evidence directly into national policy conversations.

Anchor actionPresent the Education Thematic Programme outputs (Parent Navigation Guide, Toolkit) to federal education bodies as community-verified policy evidence.
Connection to the DecadeFrame all engagement through the Second International Decade for People of African Descent (2025–2034) — giving federal partners a recognised UN framework to support.
Technology angleIntroduce digital literacy and ed-tech access as a sub-theme, connecting educational equity to the technology pillar.
02
Head Spear Role #2 · Data & Technology Bridge

Community Data Partner for Federal Monitoring of Racial Equity

ICOPAD claims a role as the trusted community partner that collects, validates, and feeds disaggregated data on African Descent communities into federal statistical and monitoring frameworks — filling a recognised gap in national equity data.

Anchor actionPropose a formal data-collection protocol to relevant federal statistical bodies — co-designed with ICOPAD member networks — to produce community-level education and welfare indicators.
Technology anglePosition ICOPAD as a digital-first organisation that can deploy tech-enabled data tools (surveys, dashboards, community apps) — making the data partnership practical and scalable.
Connection to the DecadeUse the UN Decade's recognition, justice, and development pillars as the framework justifying disaggregated data collection at federal level.
03
Head Spear Role #3 · Community Capacity Builder

Federal Co-Implementer of the Second International Decade

ICOPAD claims the unique role of being the civil-society body that translates the Second International Decade's federal commitments into on-the-ground community programmes — bridging the gap between government declarations and real community impact.

Anchor actionFormally request a co-implementation MOU or partnership framework with the relevant federal ministry, explicitly naming the Education Thematic Programme as a pilot delivery mechanism.
Education + TechnologyPackage ICOPAD's education work and planned technology tools as a ready-made Decade implementation pilot — reducing the federal burden of direct delivery.
Immediate stepDraft a one-page "ICOPAD Decade Partnership Offer" document that any federal interlocutor can read in under three minutes — naming the three roles, the Education Programme, and the technology roadmap as concrete deliverables.

Next Step: The Partnership Offer Document

The most immediate action is drafting a concise one-pager — the "ICOPAD Decade Partnership Offer" — that names these three roles and links them to concrete deliverables already underway (the Education Programme, this document hub, and the planned technology roadmap).

About

About ICOPAD

The Independent Council for People of African Descent works to advance recognition, justice, and development for African and African Descent communities, in alignment with the UN's International Decade for People of African Descent.

Our Focus

ICOPAD's primary thematic focus is on education and technology — two areas where structured community engagement can generate lasting impact for families and future generations.

The Second Decade

All ICOPAD programmes are framed within the Second International Decade for People of African Descent (2025–2034), providing a recognised UN framework for community engagement with federal governments.
